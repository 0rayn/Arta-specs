### **Why Arta Exists**
*   **The Universe Setting:** Society has fractured into decentralized enclaves after the "Global Warning" and the "Great Log-off," leaving hardware hackers called Tarbits to survive on Old World e-waste.
*   **The Threats:** Autonomous Martas AI colony systems went into "Read-Only" lockdown, classifying any unregistered human as a threat to be eliminated by drones.
*   **The Genesis of Arta:** The language was engineered for two survival reasons:
    *   *Acoustic Camouflage:* Spoofing AI audio sensors by mimicking the ambient noise of broken hardware rather than melodic human speech.
    *   *Bandwidth Crisis:* Stripping away linguistic bloat for hyper-efficient, fast commands over static-heavy radios.
*   **The Uptime Hierarchy:** Society is strictly divided by proximity to bare-metal logic (1. The Architects, 2. The Ops/Hackers, 3. The Splicers/Modders, 4. The Swappers/Fixes, 5. The Peripherals).

### **The Phonetic Inventory**
*   **The Hardware Consonant Classes:** Sounds are designed to spoof machine audio sensors. 
    *   *Relays & Switches (Ejectives and Clicks):* `[tʼ]`, `[kʼ]`, `[qʼ]`, `[ǀ]` (mimic mechanical switches and relays).
    *   *Static & Venting (Voiceless Fricatives):* `[s]`, `[ʃ]`, `[x]`, `[f]`, `[h]` (mimic white noise and gas leaks).
    *   *Failing Motors (Trills):* `[r]`, `[ʁ]` (mimic failing hardware and friction).
    *   *Grid Hums (Monotone Sonorants):* `[m]`, `[n]`, `[ŋ]` (mimic electrical whines and drones).
*   **The "Dead Pitch" Rule (Vowels):** True vowels vibrate vocal cords and alert the AI. In Arta, vowels are whispered "Power States".
    *   `[i]` (High State)
    *   `[ə]` (Neutral State)
    *   `[u]` (Low State)

### **The Machine Grammar**
*   **Dual-Mode Phonetics:** 
    *   *Tactical Mode:* Devoiced and whispered in the Wastes to bypass sensors.
    *   *Hearth Mode:* Voiced with true resonant vowels, used only in safe enclaves as the ultimate sign of trust.
*   **Absolute Morphology:** Strict 1:1 mapping with absolutely no silent letters.
*   **Base-16 Mathematics (The Hex-Hand):** Counting is strictly Hexadecimal. Tarbits count to 16 on one hand using the thumb as a cursor across four touch-points on the remaining four fingers.
*   **Stateful Execution & Context Switching:** Syntax strictly follows Opcode-Register-Register structure. Sentences do not require a subject attached to every action; instead, a "Thread Context" pointer sets the subject until a new context takes over.
*   **The Execution Queue (Timeline Flags):** There are no traditional tenses. Time is a system log marked by prepending the phrase with a flagged Power State:
    *   `[i.]` (Queued / Future)
    *   `[ə.]` (Active / Present)
    *   `[u.]` (Logged / Past)
*   **Logic Gates (Branching and Negation):** 
    *   `[c.]` (Compare / IF gate)
    *   `[x.]` (Invert / NOT gate)
    *   `[gh.]` (Boolean AND gate)
    *   `[r.]` (Boolean OR gate)
*   **Vector Offsets (Spatial Pointers):** Prepositions are replaced by Directional Dots placed before a target to act as an offset: `[f.]` (Inside), `[h.]` (Outside), `[s.]` (Above), `[ng.]` (Below), `[t'.]` (At/Pinned).
*   **Data Polling (Questions):** Questions are executed as GET requests by prepending the Query Flag `[q'.]` to a phrase.
*   **Operator Overloading (ALU Modifiers):** Mathematical symbols scale phrases depending on the target:
    *   `-` (Decrement): Slows an action or scales an object down.
    *   `+` (Increment): Speeds an action or scales an object up.
    *   `*` (Multiplier): Loops an action or creates an Absolute Plural for objects.
*   **Data Allocation (Possession and Demonstratives):**
    *   *Namespaces:* Placing a Context Pointer directly before a noun assigns possession (My/Our/Your/Their).
    *   *Memory Pointers:* Overloading the `[f.]` (Local) and `[h.]` (External) Vector Offsets provides "This" and "That".

### **The Scavenger's Script (Orthography)**
*   **The 7-Segment Scrape:** The written alphabet is entirely angular, repurposed from 7-segment digital LED displays for fast carving into metal.
*   **Whitespace:** Uncarved metal is treated as spaces between words.
*   **The Decimal Point (DP):** Serves two functions:
    *   *System Modifier:* When carved inside a character box, it promotes raw acoustics into System Pointers.
    *   *Execution Trigger (THEN):* When carved as a standalone box, it acts as a Hardware Bus/THEN gate concluding a condition.
*   **Glyph Architecture:** Visual shapes correspond to physical/acoustic states (e.g., Power States map to top, middle, and bottom horizontal bars).
